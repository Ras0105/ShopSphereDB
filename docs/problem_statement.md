# Business Problem

## Project Title

**ShopSphere – E-Commerce Database Management System using MySQL**

---

## Problem Statement

Modern e-commerce platforms handle a large amount of data related to customers, products, inventory, orders, payments, shipping, and reviews. Managing this information manually or through unstructured systems often leads to data redundancy, inconsistency, difficulty in tracking orders, inefficient inventory management, and poor customer experience.

The objective of ShopSphere is to design and implement a **relational database management system (RDBMS)** that efficiently manages the core operations of an e-commerce platform. The system should support product browsing, cart management, order processing, payment tracking, inventory updates, shipping management, customer reviews, and administrative operations while maintaining data integrity and reducing redundancy.

---

## Objectives

* Store and manage customer and administrator information efficiently.
* Organize products into categories for easy browsing and searching.
* Maintain inventory and stock availability of products.
* Allow customers to add products to their cart and place orders.
* Track orders, payments, and shipping status.
* Enable customers to provide product reviews and ratings.
* Support return and refund management.
* Generate analytical reports such as sales reports, low-stock products, and top-selling products.
* Ensure data integrity through primary keys, foreign keys, constraints, and normalization.

---

## Scope of the System

The ShopSphere database covers the following modules:

1. User Management
2. Product and Category Management
3. Inventory Management
4. Cart Management
5. Order Processing
6. Payment Management
7. Shipping and Delivery Tracking
8. Review and Feedback Management
9. Return and Refund Management
10. Sales and Inventory Analytics

---

## Expected Outcome

The proposed database system provides a structured and normalized schema for an e-commerce platform by decomposing the business into independent entities such as **Users, Products, Orders, Payments, Shipping, Reviews, and Inventory**. The system minimizes redundancy, maintains referential integrity, and models real-world e-commerce operations effectively using relational database concepts.

---

## Design Philosophy

```text
Real World E-Commerce Process
                ↓
Business Requirements
                ↓
Entities
                ↓
Relationships
                ↓
Normalized Tables
                ↓
Queries and Analytics
```

The project aims to demonstrate how real-world business processes can be transformed into an efficient relational database design using MySQL.
